Biography

Jiangjie Chen (陈江捷) is a third-year Ph.D. candidate at Fudan University (FDU) in School of Computer Science, Shanghai, China, where he is advised by Prof. Yanghua Xiao at Knowledge Works Lab. He is also currently a research intern at ByteDance AI Lab, where he works closely with Prof. Lei Li (now Assistant Professor at UCSB), Dr. Hao Zhou (now Assistant Professor at Tsinghua University) and Dr. Changzhi Sun.

He is a zealous believer in reasoning over language and dedicated to make neural models right for the right reasons. His research interests mainly include: 1) Language Reasoning: achieving human-like explainable machine reasoning over natural language, 2) Language Generation: building factual, controllable and knowledge-guided natural language generation systems, and 3) particularly the intersection of them. His previous research experiences also include Knowledge Acquisition.

Research Themes

Theme I - Explainable Text Reasoning, Being Right for the Right Reasons 🤔
For humans, intuitive inferences are made every now and then. However, it would require reasons for humans to convince others and justify themselves of their inferences or decisions. How can machines better convince humans of their predictions? The key may lie in being right for the right and faithful reasons.
Feb 27, 2022
Theme II - Towards Factual, Controllable and Versatile Text Generation 🤖
The success of deep text generation is being held back by its non-factuality. How to unleash the power of text generation models while restraining them from talking gibberish? Perhaps the first step is the identification of non-factuality.
Feb 27, 2022
